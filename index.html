Transitional Legal Priority

A Transitional Assertion

Adopted by the Supreme Soviet of the Kazakh SSR on October 25, 1990. Established the supremacy of Kazakh laws over all-Union legislation while still within the Union framework.

"The Declaration was not a break — it was a negotiation. Sovereignty was claimed within a federal envelope."

The Complete Break

On December 16, 1991, the Constitutional Law on State Independence was adopted. All Union-level authority was extinguished within Kazakh territory.

"The 1991 law transformed aspirations into architecture. Kazakhstan was now a nation among nations."

Part II: Milestones
1993
First Constitution

🎯 Objective

Establish the Republic as a unitary, democratic, and secular state.

✓ Outcome

Created initial legal legitimacy, though proved insufficient for rapid market transition.

1995
Core Constitution

🎯 Objective

Overcome political deadlock and establish a strong presidential system.

🏛 Milestone

Adopted by referendum on Aug 30. Introduced bicameral Parliament.

1998-2017
Evolution

🎯 Objective

Gradual modernization and redistribution of power toward Parliament.

✓ Outcome

Layered evolution shifting functions from executive to legislative branches.

2022
New Kazakhstan

🎯 Objective

Redesign state model after Jan 2022 events; limit presidential powers.

🏛 Milestone

7-year non-renewable term, restoration of Constitutional Court.
